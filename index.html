The Story

Seven years in the making.

It started with an obsession — not with social media, but with brands. The way a name makes you feel. The way the right message makes a stranger feel like a product was made specifically for them.

At 16, I co-founded a fashion brand. No mentor. No marketing budget. No formal education. Just a product, an Instagram account, and a relentless curiosity. We reached out to celebrities directly, built real relationships, grew to 12K followers, and started seeing returning customers — people who trusted us.

Then came round two — Xelo Store, solo. Zero to 700 followers in one week. People weren't just buying, they were sending photos wearing the products, saying thank you. They treated the brand like a friend. I was building community before I knew that was a skill people get paid for.

I moved to the United States, completed the Google Digital Marketing certification, and now I'm building Kaskt — a headwear brand and a live case study that proves everything I know in practice.

Imagine a store inside a private house. No sign. No address. The best product in the world — and nobody can find it. That's a business without marketing.

How I think

Marketing philosophy.

01

Marketing is a bridge.

Great products fail every day because nobody can find them. Imagine a store inside a private house — no sign, no address. That's a business without marketing. I build the bridge between what you made and the person who needs it.

02

Relationships over reach.

At 16 I was negotiating with celebrities for partnerships. Not because I had a big budget — because I understood that real relationships outlast any paid placement. People don't buy from brands. They buy from people they trust.

03

Data tells the story.

Every instinct I developed has a number behind it. I'm driven by analyzing what's working, understanding why, and making decisions that move the result. Marketing without measurement is just guessing.
